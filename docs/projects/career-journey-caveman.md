# UGG STORY OF MAGIC ROCK MAKER
### Caveman Summary of Very Long Career

---

Me make magic rocks talk to each other for thirty years.
Many tribes. Many problems. Much grunting.
This is story.

---

## Big Fire Tribe (Microsoft — First Time)

First job: biggest tribe in land. Everyone know Big Fire Tribe. They make magic thinking box talk to all other magic thinking boxes.

Me help when thinking boxes break. Many thinking boxes break. Me fix. Me fix again. Me fix all day.

Then chief say: "You. Go make sure new magic rock work before we give to village." Me say okay. Me smash magic rock many times to see if break. If break, me tell tribe. If not break, tribe give to village. This good system.

Me learn much at Big Fire Tribe. Big Fire Tribe very smart. Me carry lessons forever.

---

## Number Counting Insurance Cave (Transamerica)

New tribe. They count risk. Big numbers. Very complicated.

Me build special magic rock that help tribe count who owe what to who when bad thing happen. Insurance tribe have many rules. Me learn rules. Me put rules in magic rock. Magic rock count correctly.

Chief pleased. Me pleased. Onto next cave.

---

## Long Work Cave — Seventy Hours! (InfoSystem NC)

This cave change everything.

Chief say: "We make magic rock for trade place — where village swap shiny things for goods." Me join tribe. Eight rock-makers. Me think: "This fine."

THEN: chief say stay. Work more. Work much more. Work seventy hours every sun cycle. Me work. Tribe work. Everybody work. Magic rock get very big — five thousand scratches on cave wall! Magic rock win BIG AWARD from Big Fire Tribe. Most advanced trade-rock in all land!

But also: tribe leader not treat rock-makers good. Not treat customers good. Me win "Best New Rock-Maker" prize. Me say thank you. Then me leave.

Some caves teach you what to do. This cave teach what NOT do. Both useful. Actually second one MORE useful.

Me decide: work for self now. No more bad chief. ME choose who me work for.

## Duke Energy Big Power Cave (SMCI / Duke Energy)

First self-work job. Big Power Tribe control fire for whole region. Very big. Very slow. Move like mammoth in mud — deliberate, cannot be hurried.

Me help Power Tribe teach other tribe members through magic thinking box instead of big gathering. Make learning rocks. Make training rocks. Also help move big people-counting rock to new people-counting rock (PeopleSoft — fancy name for clay tablet upgrade).

Learn important lesson: big tribe have many chiefs. Must make ALL chiefs happy. This very tiring. But good skill.

## Fast Shiny Rock Counting Cave (Rabar Market Research)

This one wild.

UPS man bring GIANT thinking rock and magic sky dish to home cave. Tribe say "We send you tools. You work from home cave. Move shiny rocks very fast using sky dish."

Chief of tribe — Paul Rabar — very wise man. Long ago, wise teacher teach him secret of trading: follow pattern, stay discipline, no feelings, only rules. Paul take this teaching and build whole tribe around it. Tribe move nearly ONE BILLION shiny rocks around world every day.

Me and tribe watch three hundred most active shiny rock piles every night. Next morning: pounce on ones moving fastest. System run many strategies same time. Some hit. Some not hit. Ones that hit — TRADE THOSE MORE. Ones that not hit — slow down. Very smart hedge. Like betting on many cave paintings, knowing at least one will be picked by village chief.

Me build whole system in C++ — fancy rock-carving language. Also build same system for different kind of shiny rocks (equities version).

ALSO: me do all this while having baby mammoth — I mean, children — and mate at home cave. Must be good rock-maker AND good cave-father at same time. Very hard balance. Me manage.

2003: small recession come. Chief say remote rock-making over. Pack up sky dish. Me say thank you, Paul. Very good chief. Always polite. Always fair.

Now me must learn NEW magic rock language: C# and dot-net. Onto next cave.

Small cave. Important cave.

Tribe take blood numbers from healer cave. Me make rock that grab numbers, change format, send to other place. Numbers must be exactly right. Wrong numbers mean bad medicine decisions.

Me make numbers right. Me leave. Simple. Good.

## Big Fire Tribe Again (Microsoft — Second Time)

Big Fire Tribe call again. "Come help when rock-makers stuck."

Rock-maker from other cave have broken magic rock. Cannot fix. Send to Big Fire Tribe for help. Me look at broken rock. Me understand broken rock. Me explain to rock-maker why broken and how fix.

Do this all day. Every day. Very many broken rocks. Me never tire of puzzle. Each broken rock different. Each broken rock teach something.

Learn to walk into stranger's cave and understand their stuff very fast. This skill very useful forever.

## Phone Helper Cave Mess (First Union)

Big bank tribe have problem. Call center cave look like archaeological disaster. Old rocks from many different eras all piled together — console rocks, Smalltalk rocks, COBOL rocks from before me born. Agent must remember which old rock do what. Very confusing. Many grunts of frustration.

Me job: make ONE nice new rock that talk to ALL old rocks. User see only nice new rock. Old chaos happen behind wall. Agent happy. Customer happy. Me happy.

Hard part not technical. Hard part: convince tribe members that new rock better than old rocks they memorize over many years. People very attached to old rocks even when old rocks bad.

## Insurance Quote Cave (BCF)

Thirty-five rock-makers find clever idea.

Insurance broker need quote from many tribes. Before: broker enter same customer information TEN TIMES for TEN tribes. Very tedious. Much wasted time. Many grunts.

BCF idea: enter once. Me distribute to all tribes at same time. All tribes send back number. We show comparison. DONE.

Some tribes have nice clean message-rock system (XML web service). Easy connect. Other tribes have wild west cave paintings for websites. Must screen-scrape — very delicate work, like removing cave painting without damaging wall. Sometimes wall change anyway. Much maintenance required.

Big company come later. Buy whole BCF tribe. Three chiefs (Bob, Chuck, Fred) get many shiny rocks. Ten rock-makers get... pay cut option.

Most rock-makers leave.

Startup cave feel good — close, fast, everyone know everyone. But lesson learned: feeling good and getting shiny rocks not always same thing.

## Rocket Man Math Cave (Bank of America)

Trading floor cave. Very loud. Many excitable traders. 

But most interesting person: George. George used to make actual rockets for NASA sky tribe. Then George notice: bank tribe pay MORE than sky tribe. George switch. George bring rocket-brain to bank cave.

George have idea called "defeasance." Me try explain in caveman words:

Bank have very big loan. Tens of millions of shiny rocks. Bank not want to pay all at once (complicated reasons — tax wolf, book-keeping mammoth). Instead: bank buy government promise-rocks (T-bills, T-notes, T-bonds). Promise-rocks pay out slowly over time. Payment from promise-rocks cover monthly loan payment. EXACTLY cover. Not too little (penalty wolf attack). Not too much (tax mammoth sit on you).

Tricky part: balance change every month. Interest count every day. Must always have right combination of short promise-rocks and long promise-rocks to stay in safe zone. For. Entire. Life. Of. Loan.

Loan portfolio size: $236 BILLION shiny rocks.

Me build magic rock. Algorithm go two hundred, three hundred levels deep to find optimal answer. Very memory-hungry rock. But work.

Team size: two cave-people. Me and George.

Two cave-people. $236 billion shiny rocks. Me still proud of this.

Contract end clean. Tribe trained. Knowledge transferred. George and me nod. Good work done.

## Many Client Consulting Cave (Pegasystems)

Big consulting tribe say: "Come. Train in Boston cave. Get certified. Then go fix other people's cave problems. Repeat."

Training: two moons in Boston. Very much to learn. Pega platform = "situational layered cake." Big configurable magic rock. Can shape to almost any problem. Low code — but still need smart rock-maker to configure properly. Many architects. Many consultants. Expensive. Powerful.

Once certified: become Pega Systems Architect. Fancy title. Real meaning: do EVERYTHING. Requirements. Building. Testing. Change management. Support. Sometimes also pretend-sales. All of it. Every client.

**CIBC (Toronto):** Fly to Toronto every week for one full year. Big bank. One hundred-plus consultants all build one thing: new customer onboarding for all channels (walk-in cave, phone cave, magic internet cave). Also build AI brain that figure out which offers give to which customer at right moment. Big project. Much complexity. Much flying.

**T-Mobile (Seattle):** Very funny story. Phone repair tribe manage one million phones moving through twenty repair vendors using... EXCEL SPREADSHEET. Every phone have unique IMEI number — like personal ID cave-mark. Tracking millions of these through repair, refurb, and redistribution IN SPREADSHEET = madness. Me build actual system. Chief very relieved.

**Sprint (Kansas):** Help build campaign rock — system for making and sending promotional phone offers to new customers. Clean project. Good tribe. Done.

**US Census Bureau (Washington DC) — BIGGEST CAVE:**

Every ten years: must count every person in whole United States. First must find every cave that might have person inside. Start with old list from ten years ago. Build Google Maps comparison rock to see what change. Eighty to ninety percent of caves stay same. Rest need check.

Then: reach out to all caves. Send magic mail. Send electronic message. Build phone question system. Build internet question cave. Most people answer. Flag ones who do not.

For caves that not answer: send actual cave-walkers with tablets and GPS magic to knock on doors. Manager sit in office and use our rock to send walkers to right neighborhoods systematically. Walkers use phone rock with GPS to mark which caves visited, which person home, which cave has angry dog, which cave look unsafe. If person home and willing: ask survey questions — in their language, whatever it is.

Before system: needed 500,000 to 600,000 contractor cave-walkers.

After system: under 300,000.

MORE THAN FIFTY PERCENT REDUCTION. Me very proud of this one.

**RPA Chapter:** Pega buy robot automation company. RPA = robot that click through screens automatically so human not have to. Useful for small, specific tasks where no other way to get data. 

But: corporations abuse RPA. Use it try replace all rock-makers with cheaper no-code people. No-code people not understand logging, error handling, data types, production-readiness. Automations break in sneaky ways. Nobody notice until big problem.

Also: Pega use RPA as trick to sell expensive main product. Buy RPA → get called by consultant → "you only using half your investment!" → buy very expensive PRPC license. Foot in door strategy. Me not love this approach.

More client caves: Bank of America again (mostly project management — less exciting). BDP International Philadelphia (real-time shipping container tracking — good tribe, smart people). Blue Cross Blue Shield (trying to automate health claims — thirty to sixty steps per claim, extremely complex decision trees). Team lead there have very ambitious idea: build Excel spreadsheet that auto-translate into RPA code. Beautiful idea. Work for simple things. Collapse under weight of real complexity. Take over year. Never finish. Me leave before end.

Some ambitious ideas cannot be rescued by more time. Sometimes must admit this.

## What Thirty Years of Cave-Making Teach

Many caves. Many tribes. Many problems. Many grunts.

What me learn:

**Small tribe with right skills can move mountain.** Two cave-people move $236 billion. Eight cave-people build tool that count America fifty percent cheaper. Tribe size not determine impact.

**Bad chief make good teacher.** Seventy-hour weeks under bad chief teach more about leadership than any good chief ever could. Lesson stick forever.

**Technology just tool.** Real work always about people. How you treat tribe. How you treat customer. How you show up when hard.

**Stay curious or become fossil.** Career go from MS-DOS to AI. Every decade: entirely new magic rocks. Must always be willing to learn new scratching.

**Rocket scientist plus good coder equal very dangerous team.** Find your George. Be someone's George.

Thirty years. Still making rocks talk to each other.

Still having fun.

Ugg.
