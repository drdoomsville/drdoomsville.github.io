## Problem

In 1995, retail ran on clunky, proprietary point-of-sale systems that barely talked to
each other. Infosystem of NC bet on building a modern, multi-chain POS platform on
then-cutting-edge technology — 32-bit Visual Basic, a Microsoft SQL Server backend, and an
OLE/COM object architecture — that could serve many retail chains from a single codebase.

## What I built

I joined mid-stream and was quickly pulled in to operate at the system level on a
~5,000-line n-tier application designed to run across multiple chains simultaneously. Over
the year I was promoted to Senior Developer — the #2 engineer on the project — as the team
nearly doubled from 8 to 15. The platform became Petco's first-ever POS system and rolled
out to grocery chains including Giant Eagle, going live in 20+ stores across 5+ chains.

## Outcome

The product won Microsoft's Partner of the Year Award for the Retail Industry, and I was
named Rookie of the Year on the technical side — recognition of how much I grew and
contributed in a single intense year. It also taught me a lasting lesson that still shapes
how I work: technical success means little without a culture that treats its people well.
